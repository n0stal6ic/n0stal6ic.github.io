12/20/18 Section 10113 - Hemp Production
297A — Definitions
1. Hemp

The plant Cannabis sativa L. and any part of that plant, including seeds, derivatives, extracts, cannabinoids, isomers, acids, salts, and salts of isomers, with a delta-9 THC concentration not more than 0.3% on a dry weight basis.

2. Indian Tribe

An Indian Tribe as defined in the Indian Self-Determination and Education Assistance Act.

3. Secretary

The Secretary of Agriculture.

4. State

A State, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, or any other U.S. territory or possession.

5. State Department of Agriculture

The State government agency responsible for agriculture.

6. Tribal Government

The governing body of an Indian Tribe.

Remaining Text of the 2018 Hemp Law (Generally Unimportant for Definition)
Section 297B — State & Tribal Plans

Requirements for State or Tribal hemp regulatory plans, including licensing, land information, THC testing, disposal of non-compliant plants, enforcement, inspections, and USDA approval.

Section 297C — USDA Plan

Establishes USDA authority to regulate hemp production in States or Tribal territories without approved plans, including licensing, inspections, enforcement, and reporting.

Section 297D — Regulations & Effect on Other Law

Requires USDA to issue regulations; clarifies no effect on the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act or FDA authority.

Section 297E — Authorization of Appropriations

Authorizes funding necessary to implement the subtitle.

11/12/25 Section 781 - Hemp & Cannabinoid Definitions
Amendment Overview

Modifies Section 297A of the Agricultural Marketing Act of 1946; redefines hemp and related terms.

1. Definition of “Hemp”
A. In General

Defines hemp as Cannabis sativa L. ≤0.3% total THC dry weight.

B. Inclusion

Includes industrial hemp.

C. Exclusions
i. Viable Seeds

Seeds producing >0.3% THC plants excluded.

ii. Intermediate Products

Excludes synthetic/modified cannabinoids and >0.3% THC-equivalent intermediates.

iii. Consumer-Market Intermediate Products

Excludes intermediates sold directly to consumers.

iv. Final Products

Excludes products with non-natural cannabinoids or >0.4 mg THC-equivalent per container.

2. Definition of “Industrial Hemp”
A. Stalk & Fiber Use

Stalks and non-cannabinoid derivatives.

B. Grain & Seed Use

Grain, oil, cake, hulls, non-cannabinoid seed products.

C. Microgreens & Leaves

Microgreens and edible hemp leaves from compliant seeds.

D. Research Hemp

Hemp used only for research.

E. Viable Seed Production

Seed production for uses listed in A-D.

3. Hemp-Derived Cannabinoid Products
A. In General

Any hemp-derived product with cannabinoids for human/animal use.

B. Intermediate Products

Non-final forms intended to be mixed/formulated.

C. Container Definition

Retail-contact packaging.

D. Exclusions

Bulk shipping & non-retail packaging.

E. FDA Drug Exclusion

FDA-approved drugs excluded.

4. FDA Requirements Within 90 Days
  • List of naturally occurring cannabinoids
  • List of THC-class cannabinoids
  • List of cannabinoids with THC-like effects
  • Clarification of "container"
Comparison of 2018 vs 2025 Hemp Definitions
  • Hemp Definition: 2018 defines hemp by ≤0.3% delta-9 THC; 2025 defines hemp as ≤0.3% total THC + Acids and explicitly includes industrial hemp.
  • Scope of Products: 2025 law clarifies exclusions for synthetic cannabinoids, intermediate products, and consumer products; 2018 does not.
  • Industrial Hemp: Only defined in 2025 law with explicit categories (stalk/fiber, grain/seed, microgreens, research, viable seeds); not explicitly defined in 2018.
  • Hemp-Derived Cannabinoid Products: 2025 introduces definitions, container rules, and FDA drug exclusions; absent in 2018.
  • Regulatory Clarifications: 2025 adds explicit FDA requirements and timelines; 2018 is silent on these points.
